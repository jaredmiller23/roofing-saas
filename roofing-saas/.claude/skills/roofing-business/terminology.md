# Roofing Industry Terminology

**Purpose**: Standard industry terms used in roofing business
**Audience**: Claude (for accurate feature development)

## üìè Measurement Terms

**Square**
- Unit of measurement: 100 square feet
- Example: "20 square roof" = 2,000 sq ft
- Pricing is typically "per square" (e.g., $350/square)

**Pitch**
- Roof slope measurement: rise over run
- Format: X/12 (e.g., "6/12 pitch" = 6" rise per 12" run)
- Low pitch: 2/12 - 4/12 (walkable)
- Medium pitch: 5/12 - 7/12 (standard)
- Steep pitch: 8/12+ (requires safety equipment, more labor)

**Square Footage**
- Total roof area measured in sq ft
- Calculation: length √ó width (for each plane) + overhangs
- Tools: Measuring tape, drones, satellite imagery

## üèóÔ∏è Roofing Materials

**Shingles**
- Asphalt shingles: Most common residential (90%+)
- Architectural shingles: Thicker, premium look, 25-30 year warranty
- 3-tab shingles: Budget option, 15-20 year warranty
- Designer shingles: High-end, 40-50 year warranty

**Underlayment**
- Protective layer under shingles
- Felt (tar paper): Traditional, budget option
- Synthetic: Modern, better waterproofing, longer lasting
- Ice & water shield: Required in valleys, eaves in cold climates

**Flashing**
- Metal strips for waterproofing at joints
- **Step flashing**: Along walls, chimneys
- **Valley flashing**: Where two roof planes meet
- **Drip edge**: Along eaves and rakes
- **Pipe boots**: Around vent pipes

**Decking**
- Wooden boards under roofing material
- Plywood or OSB (oriented strand board)
- May need replacement if water-damaged

## üè† Roof Components

**Ridge**
- Highest point where two roof planes meet at the top
- **Ridge cap**: Special shingles that cover the ridge

**Valley**
- Where two roof planes meet at an angle (forms a "V")
- High water flow area, needs extra waterproofing

**Eave**
- Lower edge of the roof (where gutters attach)
- Overhangs the wall

**Rake**
- Sloped edge of a gabled roof (the diagonal edge)

**Gable**
- Triangular wall section under a peaked roof
- "Gabled roof" = two slopes meeting at a ridge

**Hip**
- External angle where two roof planes meet (not a valley)
- "Hip roof" = slopes on all four sides

**Soffit**
- Underside of the roof overhang
- Often has vents for attic ventilation

**Fascia**
- Vertical board along the roof edge (where gutters mount)

**Chimney**
- Requires special flashing, adds complexity and labor

**Skylight**
- Window in the roof
- Adds complexity, potential leak point

## üõ†Ô∏è Installation Terms

**Tear-Off**
- Removing old roofing material before installation
- "Full tear-off" = remove everything to decking
- Alternative: "Overlay" (install new shingles over old - not recommended)

**Inspection**
- Initial assessment of roof condition
- Photo documentation of damage
- Measurements for estimate

**Slope**
- Same as pitch (how steep the roof is)

**Starter Strips**
- First row of shingles at the eave
- Provides proper seal for bottom edge

**Ventilation**
- Ridge vents, soffit vents, gable vents
- Important for attic temperature, prevents ice dams
- Code requirements vary by location

**Nail Pattern**
- How many nails per shingle and where placed
- Critical for warranty compliance

## üíº Business/Sales Terms

**Lead**
- Potential customer (contact info, damage type, address)
- "Hot lead" = immediate need, high interest
- "Cold lead" = future need, low urgency

**Estimate / Quote**
- Price proposal for the work
- Itemized: materials, labor, permits, markup
- Usually valid for 30-60 days

**Contract**
- Legal agreement to perform work
- Includes: scope, price, timeline, payment terms
- Requires signature (e-signature in our app)

**Proposal**
- Similar to estimate but more detailed
- May include multiple options (budget, standard, premium)

**Inspection (Sales)**
- When sales rep visits property to assess roof
- Different from city inspector

**Walk-Through**
- Final inspection with customer after installation
- Address any concerns before final payment

**Punch List**
- Items to fix/touch up before final payment
- Example: "Clean up debris in backyard"

## üå©Ô∏è Damage Types

**Hail Damage**
- Dents/bruising on shingles
- Triggers insurance claims
- Storm targeting opportunity

**Wind Damage**
- Missing shingles, lifted edges
- Often covered by insurance
- May require emergency repairs

**Age/Wear**
- Curling, cracking, granule loss
- Not covered by insurance (maintenance issue)
- Replacement recommended

**Leak**
- Water intrusion (could be flashing, decking, shingles)
- Urgent repair needed
- May indicate larger problem

**Storm Damage**
- General term for hail, wind, falling branches
- Insurance claim likely

## üèõÔ∏è Regulatory Terms

**Permit**
- Legal approval to perform work
- Required in most municipalities
- Cost varies ($100-$500 typical in Tennessee)

**Inspection (City)**
- City/county inspector verifies code compliance
- Separate from sales inspection
- Required to close permit

**Code Compliance**
- Meeting local building codes
- Ventilation requirements, materials, installation methods
- Varies by jurisdiction

**Certificate of Completion**
- Document from city inspector
- Confirms work meets code
- Required to close permit, important for resale

## üí∞ Financial Terms

**Deposit**
- Upfront payment (typically 30%)
- Secures job, covers material costs
- Non-refundable (or subject to restocking fees)

**Progress Payment**
- Payment during job (optional, for large projects)
- Example: 40% at halfway point

**Final Payment**
- Balance due on completion (typically 70%)
- After customer walk-through, before final inspection

**Insurance Claim**
- Payment from homeowner's insurance for storm damage
- Adjuster determines payout
- Sometimes paid directly to contractor

**Markup**
- Profit margin added to costs
- Typical: 20-30% in roofing industry

**Cost Plus**
- Pricing model: actual cost + markup percentage
- Alternative to fixed price

## üë∑ Crew/Labor Terms

**Crew**
- Team of installers (typically 3-4 people)
- **Foreman**: Crew leader
- **Laborers**: 2-3 helpers

**Man-Hours**
- Total hours worked (crew size √ó hours)
- Example: 3-person crew for 8 hours = 24 man-hours

**Labor Rate**
- Hourly wage per crew member
- Typical: $75/hour in Tennessee

**Subcontractor**
- Independent contractor hired for specific work
- May handle tear-off, disposal, or full installation

## üóëÔ∏è Logistics Terms

**Dumpster**
- For disposal of old roofing material
- Rental fee ($300-$500)
- Size: 20-30 yard typical

**Delivery**
- Material delivery to job site
- Usually included in supplier quote
- Timing: 1-2 days before installation

**Tarps**
- Protect customer property during work
- Cover landscaping, vehicles, pool

**Scaffolding / Ladders**
- Access equipment for steep roofs
- Safety requirement
